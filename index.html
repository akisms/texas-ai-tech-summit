Four AI first principles

The mindset that makes everything after this work.

1

Give yourself grace

You will not keep up with everything, and you don't need to. Pick what serves your goals and let the rest go.

2

Outcomes, not AI for its own sake

Start from the result you want. The best AI use is invisible: it's the hours you got back, not the tool you used.

3

Use AI to finish, not to start

Bring your own thinking first. Let AI sharpen, expand, and accelerate. Don't let it replace your judgment.

4

Garbage in, garbage out

AI learns from what you give it. Document your context, your voice, your standards, so it can sound like you.

Level 1

Chat with *Claude*

Ask a question, get an answer. This is the level most people already live in. Let's learn to get far more out of it.

Reach for it whenyou have a one-off need: a question to answer, something to draft, summarize, brainstorm, or understand right now.

What separates a mediocre prompt from a great one

Give it a role and a goal
"You're my marketing analyst. I want X for audience Y." Context turns a generic answer into a useful one.
Show, don't just tell
Paste examples, data, a past piece in your voice. AI matches what it can see.
Iterate; it's a conversation, not a vending machine
The first answer is a draft. Refine it: "tighter," "more skeptical," "explain it for a beginner."
Ask for the format you want
Table, bullets, email, three options. Tell it the shape of the output up front.
**Pro move:** stuck on how to ask? Ask Claude to write the prompt for you. "Help me write a prompt that will get me…"

Get more out of *Chat*

Two ways past a single question: customize Claude around how you work, and use the modes most people never touch.

Customize it

Projects
A workspace that remembers your context and files, so you stop re-explaining yourself.
Skills
Reusable playbooks you teach once, then trigger on demand (a Socratic tutor, your brand-voice writer).
Connectors (MCP)
Plug Claude into your real tools (Drive, Gmail, calendar) so it works with your data.

Power moves

Use AI to understand AI
Don't know a term? Ask Claude to explain it simply, with an example from your world.
Multimodal
Drop in a screenshot, a whiteboard photo, a PDF. It sees images, and builds from them.
Voice & Artifacts
Talk to it to rehearse or roleplay; ask for a calculator or chart and watch it render live.

**These building blocks carry with you** to Levels 2 and 3. Learn them once, use them everywhere.

Level 3

Build with *Claude Code*

No coding background? No problem. Turn plain English into working tools, and unlock what that means for non-technical builders.

Reach for it whenyou want something custom that lives on its own (an app, a website), or a heavy analytical job that needs real computing power.

You don't write code. You point Claude at what you want.

Claude Code lives in the terminal, so it doesn't just write code: it runs it, installs what's needed, manages versions, and deploys the whole chain. You stay the decision-maker, and nothing happens without your permission. Here's the only vocabulary you need.

claude-code · your-project
$ project folder

One directory on your computer that holds everything you're building. Claude works inside it, so the project stays in one place.

$ permissions

You decide what Claude can do on its own versus what needs your sign-off. Your safety dial as autonomy goes up.

$ CLAUDE.md

A plain-text house-rules file. Set them globally (how you like to work) and per project (what this build needs).

$ GitHub & repos

The cloud home for what you build. A "repo" stores your project, its history, and lets you ship it to the world.

More autonomy means more responsibility

As you move up the levels, AI does more on its own. These three habits keep you in control the whole way.

Mind your data

Don't paste secrets, client-confidential info, or anything you wouldn't want stored. Know what's sensitive before you share it.

Rail: think before you paste

Verify the output

AI can sound confident and still be wrong. It "hallucinates." You finish, not it: check the facts, numbers, and claims that matter.

Rail: trust, then verify

Use permissions

The more it can act for you, the more you set boundaries. Approve big actions; let it run the safe, routine ones.

Rail: you hold the dial
**Chat**answers only, low risk
**Cowork**acts for you, set boundaries
**Code**builds and runs, permission first
